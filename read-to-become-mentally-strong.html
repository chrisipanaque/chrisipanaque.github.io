Read these 21 phrases every morning to become mentally strong.

Mental strength, as any other skill, can be developed with practice. It is the daily practice of pushing yourself to grow stronger, maintaining realistic optimism, and setting healthy boundaries.

Avoid wasting time feeling sorry for yourself or giving up control of your emotions to others. Instead, gain control of your emotions and become a stronger and better person than you were yesterday.

1. Face the fears that hold you back.

While you don't need to conquer fears because you have something to prove to others, strive to face the fears that hold you back.

2. Choose productive behavior.

While it may be tempting to make excuses, complain about other people or avoid difficult circumstances, refuse to waste time on unproductive activities.

3. Feel confident in your ability to adapt to change.

Although change is uncomfortable, it's tolerable. Focus your energy on adapting to change, rather than resisting it.

4. Balance emotions with logic.

Understand how your emotions can influence your thinking in an effort to make the best decisions possible.

5. Learn from your mistakes.

Don't hide or excuse your mistakes. Instead, learn from them.

6. Balance self-acceptance with self-improvement.

Accept yourself for who you are, while simultaneously recognizing your need for personal development.

7. Genuinely celebrate other people's success.

Cooperate and collaborate, rather than compete with those around you. Don't feel as though other people's success somehow diminishes your own achievements.

8. Be comfortable living according to your values.

Make decisions based on understanding of your priorities and the values you live by.

9. Focus on sharpening your skills, rather than showing them off.

While some people seek validation from others, avoid being concerned about gaining recognition. Instead, focus on staying inspired and motivated to become better.

10. Live an authentic life.

Be true to yourself. Your words have to be aligned with your behavior.

11. View life's hardships as opportunities for growth.

While hardship causes some people to grow bitter, learn from the adversity you faced, which made you a better person today.

12. Your self-worth depends on who you are, not what you achieve.

Whether you win or lose, your decisions, personality and life values makes who you are.

13. Practice delayed gratification.

View your goals as a marathon, not a sprint. Be willing to tolerate short-term pain when it can provide long-term gain.

14. Accept personal responsibility for your choices.

Indecision carries doubts about the outcomes of our decisions and fear of failure. After making a decision, accept complete responsibility for your actions.

15. Bounce back from failure.

Don't view failure as the end of your journey. Instead, use your failed attempts as learning opportunities where you gained knowledge, which will increase your chances of success in the future.

16. Engage in active problem-solving.

Don't dwell on problems, create solutions instead.

17. Express gratitude.

Rather than exclaim you need more, acknowledge you have more than you need, and appreciate all that is around you.

18. Focus on what you can control.

Devote and focus all your resources to the things you can control. Do not waste it on things you can't.

19. Be open to learning more from all that surrounds you.

Constantly learn from your circumstances, and the people you encounter every day.

20. Work on your weaknesses, rather than masking them.

Do not waste your resources to disguise your vulnerabilities, instead, invest all of your energy into improving yourself.

21. Be a realistic optimist.

Always maintain a positive attitude. Bring hope and display confidence in all of your hard work but don't allow your optimistic tendencies derive you from focusing on the problems you are currently facing.
